Part 4: Passwords

Passwords are on the frontline of keeping nearly all of your online presence safe. On most sites a password is the only thing preventing an attacker from accessing all of your information. Some sites have opted for two-factor authentication methods, which are meant to supplement the login process by requiring the user to respond to a text or email along with the password to allow entry. We highly suggest using two-factor authentication when available as well. Below you can follow our guidelines on how to generate a strong password that can't be guessed and use our own password strength indicator to precheck if a password is safe. All functions on this site are encrypted and/or done in your browser, meaning that your password cannot be itercepted through transmission or in our servers.

Password Strength Indicator

  • Don't use any dictionary words.
  • Include a mix of capital letters, lowercase letter symbols and numbers.
  • Long Length (12-14 characters).
  • Two-factor authentication.
  • Update passwords every 6 months.
  • Do not reuse passwords.
  • Use different passwords for different sites.
  • Do not share your password.

Data Breaches

Data breaches are malicious attacks on known sites with the intent of stealing and exposes the information and data of its users. This information includes credit card numbers, names, email addresses, physical addresses, and other sensitive data. Attackers will use sophisticated methods such as those mentioned in Part 3 to breach into websites and systems. Websites do take precaution when hosting your sensitive information. When possible most secure sites will encrypt most of their users data so that only the user can decrypt but attackers have methods in order to decrypt that data. Data breaches are very common, and due to the nature of how easily it is to make an online presence you may find that your information might have been exposed in attack that has been publicly documented.
